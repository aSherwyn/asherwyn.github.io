About Me

Hi! I'm Asa, a data analyst with a background in project management, health & wellness, and arts education. 10 years ago, amid an icy Alaska winter, I had my first confused adventure in data investigation. What began as a relentless lesson in corrupt data evolved into a decade of discovery and learning. Today I delight in parsing together seemingly incongruous pieces of information and distilling droves of data into actionable insights.

Currently, I am open to remote or local data analysis work. My fortes are data cleaning, investigation, and visualization. My tools of choice are Python and Tableau, and I also have experience in SQL and Microsoft Excel.

My preferred genres of data lie in health and wellness, education, and research – though I am admittedly eager for a wide variety of challenges! If you’re looking for a creative problems solver with a knack for clear, compassionate communication to join your team, don’t hesitate to reach out.

Intro

GameCo, an imaginary enterprise, wanted an analysis of their rival companies and current video game trends to give them a competitive edge in the industry. A descriptive analysis resulted in a report featuring key, actionable insights for the company.

Cleaning

This analysis used Microsoft Excel to clean and explore
the data. Exploratory visualizations revealed chaotic
early years in the video game industry from 1980 to 1995.
Filtering these years out of the analysis allowed for a
clearer and more current understanding of the data.

Data

  • Obtained from www.VGChartz.com.
  • VGChartz records data for video games with 100,000 or more unit sold from 1980-2016.
  • After removing incomplete entries 16,590 remained.
  • Each entry has data for game title, platform, year released, genre, publisher, North American sales, European sales, Japanese sales, Other Sales, and Global sales.
  • Years 1980-1993 were filtered out to remove outdated data incongruous with current trends.
  • Values were compared as total sales per region and as percentage of global sales by region.
